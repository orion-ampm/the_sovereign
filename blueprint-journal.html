The Blueprint
Journal

90 days. 90 prompts. One woman, becoming sovereign — one honest answer at a time. Write here, or print and use your own journal. The words are what matter.

Phase 1 — Clarity (Days 1–30)
Before you can build, you must see clearly. These prompts are about truth — financial, emotional, and personal. They may be uncomfortable. That discomfort is the beginning of everything.
Week 1 — Days 1–7Facing the numbers
Day 1
What is your relationship with money — honestly, in your own words?
Not what it should be. What it actually is right now.
Day 2
What financial truth have you been avoiding?
The number you don't look at. The account you don't check. Name it.
Day 3
What did your parents teach you about money — explicitly or by example?
Day 4
Where has financial dependence cost you something important?
Day 5
What is the invisible tax you have paid in your life? Put a number on it.
Use the Invisible Tax Calculator if you haven't already.
Day 6
If money had nothing to do with love or security — how would you spend it differently?
Day 7
Week 1 reflection: What was the hardest truth this week? What will you do with it?
Week 2 — Days 8–14Understanding your position
Day 8
What do you actually own — in your name, unambiguously yours?
Day 9
What are you owed — financially, professionally, personally — that you have not claimed?
Day 10
What does financial independence mean to you — not in theory, but in daily lived experience?
Day 11
What financial decision are you most proud of? What did it teach you?
Day 12
What financial decision do you most regret? What would you tell a younger woman about it?
Day 13
Who has benefited financially from your unpaid or underpaid labor? Name them.
Day 14
Week 2 reflection: What shift has happened in how you see your financial position?
Weeks 3–4 — Days 15–30Rewriting the story
Day 15
What story about women and money were you told that you no longer believe?
Day 17
What would you do in the next 12 months if money was not a limiting factor?
Day 20
Describe the woman you are becoming financially — in the present tense, as if she already exists.
Day 23
What is the one financial action you have been delaying that you will take this week?
Day 28
What does sovereignty mean to you — beyond money? What does it feel like in your body?
Day 30
Phase 1 complete. What is the most important thing you now know that you didn't 30 days ago?
Phase 2 — Protection (Days 31–60)
Clarity without protection is incomplete. These prompts move you from seeing your position to actively securing it — legally, financially, and personally.
Days 31–45Securing what you have
Day 31
What are you most afraid of losing? What would it take to protect it?
Day 34
What documents do you need to get in order this month? Name the three most urgent.
Use the Asset Protection Checklist as your reference.
Day 37
If your income stopped tomorrow, what would your first 7 moves be?
Day 40
What hard asset do you want to own by the end of this year, and what is your plan?
Day 44
Who would you call in a financial emergency right now? If the answer is no one — what would it take to change that?
Day 45
Mid-phase reflection: What protection action have you taken in the last two weeks?
Days 46–60Building the fortress
Day 47
What financial structure could protect your assets from any future relationship ending?
Day 50
What does your emergency fund look like? What would make you feel genuinely safe?
Day 54
Write a letter to your future self — from a place of complete financial sovereignty. What does she want you to know?
Day 58
What is the single most important thing you have put in place in the last 30 days to protect yourself?
Day 60
Phase 2 complete. You are more protected than you were 60 days ago. What does that feel like?
Phase 3 — Building (Days 61–90)
The final phase. You have clarity. You have protection. Now you build — with intention, with strategy, and with the knowledge that what you are creating is entirely yours.
Days 61–75Designing your wealth
Day 61
What does your ideal financial life look like in 5 years — in complete detail?
Day 64
What income stream could you realistically build in the next 12 months that doesn't trade purely your time?
Day 67
What role does gold play in your long-term wealth strategy? Be specific.
Day 70
What financial habits would you need to build — and break — to reach your 5-year vision?
Day 73
Who are the women in your life who embody financial sovereignty? What can you learn from them?
Day 75
Mid-phase reflection: What is the biggest financial shift you have made in 75 days?
Days 76–90Becoming the blueprint
Day 78
What would you tell the woman you were 90 days ago?
Day 81
What financial legacy do you want to leave — for your children, your community, or the women who come after you?
Day 85
You began this journal at a financial confidence level. Where are you now? What moved?
Day 88
What is the one financial commitment you are making for the next 90 days?
Day 90
Journal complete. You chose to begin. You chose to continue. Write to the woman who is about to start Day 1 — what does she need to know?

Your journal is private. Nothing is stored or transmitted. All responses exist only in this browser session.
