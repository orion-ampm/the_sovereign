Gold Investment
Starter Checklist

Eight years of industry knowledge, distilled into the exact steps a woman needs to buy, hold, and protect gold correctly — from the very first purchase.

0 of 24 completed0%
Gold has preserved wealth for over 5,000 years. It doesn't answer to interest rate decisions, company earnings, or political cycles. For a woman building an unbreakable financial foundation — it belongs in your portfolio.
01
Know before you buy
Understanding what you're investing in — and why
✓
Understand the spot price
The spot price is the current market price per troy ounce. Check it daily at kitco.com or goldprice.org. Everything is priced relative to this.
✓
Understand premiums
You always pay above spot price. Premiums cover minting, dealer margin, and distribution. Coins carry higher premiums than bars. Know what premium you're paying.
✓
Know the four main forms of gold
Physical (bars/coins), ETFs, mining stocks, futures. For a woman building real protection — physical gold is the only form no one can freeze, seize, or dilute.
✓
Decide your allocation target
Most financial advisors suggest 5–15% of your portfolio in gold. For women in financial rebuilding — starting at 10% is a strong foundation.
✓
Understand gold's role in your portfolio
Gold is not a get-rich instrument. It is a store of value, a hedge against inflation and currency collapse, and insurance against crisis. Think decades, not months.
02
Your first purchase
Buying correctly from the very beginning
✓
Choose physical gold for your first purchase
Start with something you can hold. 1oz gold coins (Britannia, Maple Leaf, American Eagle) are liquid, recognizable worldwide, and easy to store.
✓
Use a reputable dealer only
Never buy gold from private sellers, social media, or unverified sources. Use established dealers with LBMA accreditation or national mint certification.
✓
Get a certificate of authenticity
Every purchase should come with documentation: weight, purity (.999 or .9999), serial number where applicable. Keep this forever.
✓
Compare at least 3 dealers before buying
Premiums vary significantly. A 0.5% difference on a $2,000 purchase is $10 — on larger purchases, comparison saves real money.
✓
Record your purchase price and date
Keep a simple spreadsheet: date, form, weight, spot price, premium paid, total cost, dealer. This matters for tax purposes and for tracking your real returns.
03
Storage & protection
Because owning gold means nothing if it isn't secure
✓
Never store gold at home without a proper safe
If storing at home, use a bolted, fire-resistant safe — minimum £1,000 insurance rating. Under a mattress is not storage. It's a gift to whoever finds it.
✓
Consider vault storage for larger holdings
Professional allocated storage (BullionVault, The Royal Mint, Brinks) is insured, audited, and fully segregated. Annual fees are typically 0.1–0.5% of value.
✓
Insure your physical gold
Check whether your home insurance covers precious metals — most standard policies have a £500–1,500 limit. A specialist rider or separate policy is worth the cost.
✓
Tell only who needs to know
Your gold holdings are private. There is no reason for anyone outside your legal/financial team to know what you hold. Discretion is part of security.
04
Building your position over time
Making gold a consistent part of your wealth strategy
✓
Set a regular buying schedule (dollar cost average)
Buying the same amount monthly regardless of price smooths out volatility. You buy more when prices are low, less when high. Over time this builds position efficiently.
✓
Diversify your gold forms as your holding grows
Start with coins for liquidity. Add bars (lower premiums) as holdings grow. Consider allocated vault storage for holdings above $10,000.
✓
Review your position annually
Is gold still 10–15% of your portfolio? Has the price moved significantly? Annual rebalancing keeps your allocation intentional, not accidental.
✓
Understand when — and how — to sell
Gold is long-term. But when you do sell: use a reputable dealer, check buyback rates (aim for 98–99% of spot), and document the sale for tax purposes.
✓
Include gold in your estate / legacy planning
Physical gold passes outside of standard financial systems. Ensure your will or trust documents reference your holdings and their location. Tell your executor.
You don't need to understand every market cycle to own gold wisely. You need to buy real, store it safely, and hold it with intention. That is the entirety of the strategy that has worked for 5,000 years.
