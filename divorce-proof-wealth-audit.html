The Divorce-Proof Wealth Audit

What is yours.
What is not.

A guided map of what you actually own, what you'd lose tomorrow, and exactly what to do about it — before you need to.

This is not legal advice. Freya is not a lawyer. This audit gives you the questions a lawyer would ask, so you walk into that conversation already knowing your own answers.

Section 01
What you hold
Not what's shared. Not what's promised. What is, right now, provably yours.
Is your home — or the home you live in — in your name?
Do you have savings, investments, or accounts that are yours alone — not joint, not shared?
Do you own any physical, hard assets — gold, jewelry of real value, property — independent of any market or institution?
If you had to prove what you brought into this relationship financially — could you, with documents?
Section 02
What you'd need
If everything changed tomorrow — not someday, tomorrow — what would actually sustain you.
If your relationship ended this month, how long could you financially sustain yourself and any children, without depending on the other person?
Is your income — or your name — on file with your country's tax authority, independent of your partner's filing?
Do you know — concretely — what your legal entitlements would be in your country or region if this relationship ended?
Section 03
Who else depends on this
If there are children, this section matters more than any other.
Do you have children with this partner, or stepchildren in your care?
Is custody, schooling, and day-to-day care arrangement something you've discussed and would feel confident standing on if things changed?
Section 04
In your own words
Freya reads this. Word for word. This is where the audit becomes yours and no one else's.
What is the one thing you are most afraid you'd lose?
What is one action — even a small one — you could take this week to protect yourself, regardless of how things go?
